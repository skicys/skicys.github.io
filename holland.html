The Windmills of Holland

The windmills of Holland are a key part of Dutch heritage and a symbol of the country's enduring history of water management and land reclamation. There are about 1,200 windmills still standing in the Netherlands today, with the famous ones located at Kinderdijk and Zaanse Schans. These iconic structures were originally used for pumping water, grinding grain, and sawing wood. One of the most famous windmills is the Molen de Gooyer in Amsterdam. The windmills at Kinderdijk were built around 1740 and are a UNESCO World Heritage site. These mills played a crucial role in controlling floods and ensuring that land could be used for farming and habitation.


Dutch Cheese Markets

The Netherlands is famous for its cheese, particularly Gouda and Edam. Dutch cheese markets are a great way to experience this cheese culture. The most famous cheese markets are held in Gouda, Alkmaar, and Edam. In these traditional cheese markets, large wheels of cheese are bought and sold in a lively ceremony that dates back hundreds of years. These markets are not only a place to buy cheese but also a social and cultural event that attracts tourists from around the world. The market at Alkmaar dates back to 1593, making it one of the oldest cheese markets still in existence today.
