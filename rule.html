Main Rules of Yu-Gi-Oh!

Overview

Yu-Gi-Oh! is a trading card game where players use various cards to battle against each other. The game is played using Monster, Spell, and Trap Cards to reduce your opponent's Life Points from 8000 to 0.

Game Structure

  • Life Points: Each player starts with 8000 Life Points.
  • Deck: The Main Deck must have 40-60 cards. You can also have a 15-card Extra Deck and a Side Deck with up to 15 cards.
  • Win Conditions: You win by reducing your opponent’s Life Points to 0, making your opponent run out of cards, or achieving a special victory condition stated on a card.

Phases of a Turn

  • Draw Phase: Draw 1 card from your Deck.
  • Standby Phase: Resolve card effects that activate in this phase.
  • Main Phase 1: You can Normal Summon, set monsters, or activate Spell and Trap cards.
  • Battle Phase: Attack your opponent’s monsters or Life Points directly.
  • Main Phase 2: After the Battle Phase, you can set more cards or change monster positions.
  • End Phase: End your turn and resolve any effects that activate in the End Phase.

Additional Rules

  • Field Limitations: Each player has 5 Monster Zones and 5 Spell/Trap Zones. There is also a separate Extra Monster Zone and Pendulum Zones.
  • Card Effects: Some cards have continuous effects, while others have activation conditions that must be met.
  • Battle Damage: When a monster attacks, the result can be damage to Life Points, destruction of a monster, or no damage at all depending on the positions of the monsters.
